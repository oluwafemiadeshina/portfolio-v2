Lordtech

This is a Utility bill payment company that provides users with faster and cheaper means of purchasing Data Top-up, Airtime Top-up, Electricity bill, and Cable subscription.

Role

Research, wireframes, prototypes, UI Design, User flows, UI/UX Designer

Platform

Andriod & iOS

Duration

2 weeks

Sector

Utility bills payment

Project Overview

Background

User’s usually have challenges of purchasing bills, Lordtech is an online platform that enables its users to interactively and easily pay bills, purchase airtime and other utility bills.

Role & Responsibilities

My role is to design a usable product for users to effectively pay and manage utility bills. I conducted user interviews, user research survey, information architecture, digital wireframes, low & high fidelity protoytying, conducting usability studies, iterating on designs, defining UX metrices, and defining busniess KPI.

Problem

The users find it difficult to manage their utility bills and exiting platforms for bills payments made it difficult for users to purchase & navigate through the app, and users couldn’t schedule bills, they had to repeat similar bills frequenctly.

Project Vision / Goal Statement.

The goal

Our bill payment app will let users easily purchase and manage utility bills which will affect how users process & manage their utility bills, by giving them the ability to track, and schedule utility bills. We will measure effectiveness by analizing the number of utility bills been processed.

Project research

User research: summary

I conducted interviews to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working students & adults who had difficulty purchasing, tracking and scheduling utility bills. This user group confirmed initial assumptions about lordtech customers, but research also revealed that they’ll love to get notified when a utility bill is about to expire or renewed.

User Pain points

I learned alot about user problems during the research and found that users have similar behavior and pain points. Organizing everything helped me narrow it down to some points, from which I creaete a list of features that can help users.

Scheduling Bills
Users need an app that can provide them with a scheduling feature, to schedule utility bills that they use regularly and not get to keep repeating the same payment process severals times.

Complex Navigation
Users will not engage with anything hard to use or understand. Simplicity is one thing, but portraying value-added functionality while retaining simplicity is the main challenge. Here I used the easy navigation, menu bars to increase the engagement with the lordtech app.

Bad visual design
Users need an app that is visually appealing, as it passed a sence of trust and calmness.

Accessibility
Platforms for purchasing utility bills are not usually equipped with assistive technologies.

No support
Platforms for purchasing utility bills lack any form of support system for users when an isssue is bieng face by users.

Personas

Creating user personas helped me understand who is a product’s users, what their goals and frustrations, and for what purpose they will use the product. The result should be believable people with their own experience and values.

Discover

Insights

After conducting surveys, compiling user personas, defining target users, analyzing the responses, I developed insights that help me determine what features should be in a mobile.

Easy to schedule bills

Users need an app that can immediately provide them with a schedulling feature so they don’t repeat regular tasks & also get reminders on the app.

Simplified Navigation system

Users need an app that they can easily navigate from one feature to the other, without checking the internet on how to perform tasks on the app.

Online support

Users need an app that can immediately provide them with online support when they experience any diffculty or issuse while purchasing utility bills.

App wallet system

Users need an app that can immediately provide them easy access to services bills without experiencing payment issues. Using the wallet system help users keep money in their wallet for easy access.

Test Phase

Usability study: findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings

Users want to login to the app quickly.

Users want more login options.

Users want to be able to make withdrawals funds easily.

Round 2 findings

Users were unfamiliar and confused with the “port number” term.

Users want a better navigation menu.

Users expressed their sucureity concerns towards their the use of their apple and google authenticantion.

Design Iteration

Implementing Feedback

The suggestions from the usability testing were considered and the following changes were made.

Takeaway

Early designs had only one method of registration, but after the first usability study, I added two (2) additional options to Login to the app to simplify the sign in & sign up process for users however, due to security conderns from participants I had to refactor the signin screen to use the biometric login as an alternative which users feel confortable with.

Takeaway

Early designs didn’t make provision for users to make withdrawals which users found tricky, so I added the withdraw button to aid usres make withdrawals from their wallet.

Takeaway

The second usability study revealed frustration with the purchase utility service flow. To address the issue, I refactored the screen by removing the “Port number”, since it was less significant.

Conclusion

What did I learn?

Designing the app has been a challenging and satisfying journey, During the development of this project, I realized how important the research stage is, it helped define series of user painpoints and which to prioritize.
It was a challenge to conduct the user research and prepare the whole flow to create a complete prototype-friendly app with smooth and clear usability and, most important of all, accessibility to diverse user groups.
I’ve learnt that choices of colours, typography, and components individually and as a group-impacts the users’ experience.

What are the next steps?

I will be conducting more research to understand what will be required to flesh out the functionalities and structure of the web version of the app which caters to users who many have easy access to their desktop, in order to improve the user experience for users.
